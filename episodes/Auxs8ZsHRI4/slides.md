# 为什么这期值得听

<div class="grid grid-cols-3 gap-3 mt-4 text-sm">

<div class="p-3 rounded bg-blue-50 border-l-4 border-blue-500">

### 一个奇怪的交集

临床心理学家、育儿权威被请上产品经理的播客——主持人的假设是办公室里的成年人更像婴儿。

</div>

<div class="p-3 rounded bg-green-50 border-l-4 border-green-500">

### 一套理论两处用

Dr. Becky：所有人从 1 岁到 85 岁都需要同样的东西。一套原则既适用于 2 岁，也适用于 40 岁。

</div>

<div class="p-3 rounded bg-orange-50 border-l-4 border-orange-500">

### 几个硬核工具

repair、MGI、sturdy leader、boundary 的精确定义、"I believe you + I believe in you"——全是可以照抄的话术。

<div class="p-3 rounded bg-purple-50 border-l-4 border-purple-500">

### 一个反直觉的结论

Optimizing for happiness 在童年反而**造就**成年的 anxiety 和 fragility。同理适用工作团队。

<div class="p-3 rounded bg-red-50 border-l-4 border-red-500">

<div class="p-3 rounded bg-yellow-50 border-l-4 border-yellow-500">

# 先把前提摆出来

<div class="mt-4 text-sm opacity-70">Lenny 的引入（引用另一位嘉宾 Ryas Doshi 的观察）：</div>

<div class="mt-4 p-5 rounded border-l-4 border-orange-500 bg-orange-50 bg-opacity-40 italic text-lg">

"Most adults in the corporate environment are really just babies in disguise."

</div>

<div class="mt-4 text-sm opacity-70 not-italic">
会议上要抢 credit、不肯分预算、不让自己喜欢的项目被砍、要别人帮自己解决问题。"他们看上去是大人，但其实是婴儿。"
</div>

<div class="mt-6 p-5 rounded border-l-4 border-green-500 bg-green-50 bg-opacity-40">

**Dr. Becky 的"最大善意解读"回应：**

"all humans need the same things. Whether we're one or five or 45 or 85"——需求没变，只是随年龄改了说法。行为出问题，是需求没有被好好满足。

</div>

# 这期要用的一套坐标

<div class="grid grid-cols-2 gap-6 mt-6">

<div class="p-4 rounded bg-blue-50 border-l-4 border-blue-500">

### 两类工作

1. **设边界（boundaries）**——你决定要做的事
2. **验证情绪（validate）**——承认对方的体验是真实的

Dr. Becky: "这两件事合起来才是 sturdy leadership。大多数人只做了其中一半。"

</div>

<div class="p-4 rounded bg-green-50 border-l-4 border-green-500">

### 三个操作工具

- **repair**：事后回头修补
- **MGI**：most generous interpretation
- **connect before correct**：先连接，再纠正

</div>

<div class="p-4 rounded bg-orange-50 border-l-4 border-orange-500">

### 两个底层假设

- 人都是 **good inside**（behavior ≠ identity）
- 坏行为 = **feelings overpower skills**

</div>

<div class="p-4 rounded bg-purple-50 border-l-4 border-purple-500">

### 一条总目标

**resilience over happiness**——长期韧性优于短期舒服。

</div>

</div>

# Repair：最核心的关系工具

<div class="mt-3 text-sm opacity-80">

**安全依恋 (secure attachment)** 的定义不是"父母从不出错"，而是有一个愿意事后回头修补的大人。

</div>

<div class="mt-3 space-y-2 text-sm">

<div class="p-2 rounded bg-red-50 border-l-4 border-red-500">

**挡在路上**：我要做一个完美的父母/领导。Dr. Becky 家：**"perfect is creepy"**。

</div>

<div class="p-2 rounded bg-blue-50 border-l-4 border-blue-500">

**Repair 三步**：① 承认自己那一部分 → ② 说出对方受到的影响 → ③ 说下次会怎么做

</div>

<div class="p-2 rounded bg-green-50 border-l-4 border-green-500">

**工作版本**："hey, earlier in the meeting, I totally cut you off. I used a really harsh tone... I'm sorry. I'm going to work on that."

</div>

</div>

# Connect before correct：报税的故事

<div class="mt-4 grid grid-cols-2 gap-6">

<div>

### 场景 A（失败版）

你刚坐下来看书，配偶走过来：

> "Becky, we have to do our taxes."

你："Whoa, I just sat down to read a book."

他："You don't listen to me. You have a listening problem. If you don't do the taxes with me right now, I'm taking away your dessert for a week."

<div class="mt-3 text-sm opacity-70">
Dr. Becky：没人会说我有 listening problem，大家只会说我有一个 husband problem。
</div>

</div>

<div>

### 场景 B（成功版）

> "Whoa, I realize I didn't tell you this. We have to get our taxes done tonight. You look like you're just settling in to that book. Can we get on top of this together? I know we're on the same team."

<div class="mt-3 p-4 rounded bg-green-50 border-l-4 border-green-500">

"The chances that I will do taxes just skyrocketed."

**原因**：他看见我是一个完整的人，而不是"他世界里要被 get something done 的一个对象"。

</div>

</div>

</div>

<div class="mt-6 p-4 rounded bg-blue-50 border-l-4 border-blue-500">

**同样的脚本在工作环境里**：把 "我们要让你改版这个 PRD" 换成 "我看到你在赶这个 launch，我想和你一起想一下 PRD 要怎么改" —— 桥搭好了，事情才能过桥。

</div>

# 连接的真正门槛是 mindset，不是脚本

<div class="mt-4 p-5 rounded border-l-4 border-purple-500 bg-purple-50 bg-opacity-40 italic text-lg">

"we feel people's intention, not just their intervention."

</div>

<div class="mt-6 grid grid-cols-2 gap-6">

<div class="p-4 rounded bg-red-50 border-l-4 border-red-500">

### ❌ 带着 agenda 的连接

开会前花 30 秒假装关心对方长周末过得怎么样，其实心里在 count down。

Dr. Becky: **"it's going to feel dirty to you. You're going to feel it."**

</div>

<div class="p-4 rounded bg-green-50 border-l-4 border-green-500">

### ✓ 真正的连接

和孩子：把手放他背上"久到有点不自然"、"I'm happy to see you"。

和同事："Tell me a little about your long weekend"——不是在 count down 让他讲完好开始派活。

</div>

</div>

<div class="mt-4 p-3 rounded bg-orange-50 border-l-4 border-orange-500 text-sm">

**Dr. Becky 对自己的观察**："efficiency and relationship building are often in opposition."——效率型的人做关系建设会觉得"low stim"，那种"没什么事在发生"的感觉反而是做对了。

</div>

# Good Inside：把身份和行为分开

<div class="mt-4 text-sm opacity-80">

Dr. Becky 的品牌、书、公司、方法全围绕这一句话：
**"这是一个好人，ta 正在做某件不好的事。"**

</div>

<div class="mt-4 space-y-3">

<div class="p-3 rounded bg-red-50 border-l-4 border-red-500">

**我们惯常的 collapse**：
"他总是迟到 → 这个人很懒。"
"他打了妹妹 → 他是个坏小孩。"

</div>

<div class="p-3 rounded bg-green-50 border-l-4 border-green-500">

**Dr. Becky 教的分离**：
"I have a good kid who's hitting."
"This is a good person who is late."

</div>

<div class="p-3 rounded bg-blue-50 border-l-4 border-blue-500">

**为什么这重要**：只有这样分开，你才能谈"行为"本身——否则对方一旦觉得你在评判他的 identity，整场对话就变成了无意识的"我是不是一个好人"的辩论。

</div>

</div>

# 万能开场白："我们是一队的"

<div class="mt-4 text-sm opacity-70">Dr. Becky 说这是整期她最想让听众带走的一句话——</div>

<div class="mt-6 p-5 rounded border-l-4 border-blue-500 bg-blue-50 bg-opacity-40 italic text-lg">

"First of all, I want to say we're on the same team."

</div>

<div class="mt-4 text-sm opacity-70 not-italic">
"If that's the only thing people take from this, that is the most amazing way to have a more productive conversation with anyone."
</div>

<div class="mt-6 grid grid-cols-2 gap-4">

<div class="p-4 rounded bg-gray-50 border border-gray-200">

### 一个 good-inside 式的迟到对话

- "我想先说我们是一个队的。"
- "你是个好人，你也知道我们需要按时开会。"
- "但这事一直在发生，所以我知道肯定有别的事 going on。"
- "是工作里有什么让你不想来？家里有事？还是时间管理的问题？"
- "Whatever it is, let's get to the bottom of it together."

</div>

<div class="p-4 rounded bg-green-50 border-l-4 border-green-500">

### 这段话里发生了什么

1. 把 **identity 放到 off the table**（你是个好人、你也不笨）
2. 把 **behavior 摆到桌子上**（这事在反复发生）
3. 把 **归因打开**（是外部？是 skill？）

那之后才能真的谈行为。

</div>

</div>

# 防御的秘密：对话内容不是对话内容

<div class="mt-4 text-sm opacity-80">

Dr. Becky 的一个关键观察——

</div>

<div class="mt-4 p-5 rounded border-l-4 border-red-500 bg-red-50 bg-opacity-40 italic text-lg">

"you're having a debate you don't even realize you're having, which is whether they're a good, moral, worthy person."

</div>

<div class="mt-6 grid grid-cols-2 gap-6">

<div class="p-4 rounded bg-red-50 border-l-4 border-red-500">

### 表面上的对话

- 你："你这周报告做得很糙。"
- 他："我这周忙得要死还要处理 …"
- 你："那也不能糙。"
- 他："你知道我昨天加班到几点吗 …"

</div>

<div class="p-4 rounded bg-purple-50 border-l-4 border-purple-500">

### 脑子里真正在争的

- 你（心想）："这份 deliverable 不合格。"
- 他（心想）："她在说我是个不负责任的人。我必须证明我不是。"
- 行为讨论就此失焦。

</div>

</div>

<div class="mt-4 p-4 rounded bg-blue-50 border-l-4 border-blue-500">

**解法**：开头就把 identity 问题关掉。"你是个好人，你是个聪明人，我们是一队的。**我们谈的只是这一件具体的事**。"

</div>

# MGI — Most Generous Interpretation

<div class="text-sm opacity-70 mt-2">Dr. Becky 怕"没有行动的想法"，所以把 good-inside 变成睡前动作——</div>

<div class="mt-3 p-4 rounded border-l-4 border-blue-500 bg-blue-50 bg-opacity-40 italic">

"The story you tell yourself as a kid at night kind of becomes the parent you are the next morning."

</div>

<div class="mt-3 grid grid-cols-2 gap-4 text-sm">

<div class="p-3 rounded bg-red-50 border-l-4 border-red-500">

### LGI — Least Generous（默认的脑子）

"我跟他说不许在沙发上跳，他看着我的眼睛跳得更用力——**this kid's a sociopath**。"（她儿子两岁。）

</div>

<div class="p-3 rounded bg-green-50 border-l-4 border-green-500">

### MGI 版本

"这是一个 **very oriented around control** 的孩子，一旦觉得失控就 double down。'disappointed with you' 的说辞对他完全无效。"

</div>

</div>

<div class="mt-3 p-3 rounded bg-purple-50 border-l-4 border-purple-500 text-sm">

**换到工作**：某人会议上反复讲一个观点。LGI = "他在贪功"。MGI = "他可能第一次没觉得被听见"——私下说："I wonder if you don't always feel heard. **We're in a bad cycle. Can we work together on changing this?**"

</div>

---

# 为什么 MGI 不是软弱

<div class="mt-4 grid grid-cols-2 gap-4">

<div class="p-4 rounded bg-gray-50 border border-gray-200">

### 常见反应

"你是让我给混账行为找借口吗？"

</div>

<div class="p-4 rounded bg-green-50 border-l-4 border-green-500">

### Dr. Becky 的回答

"不是。behavior 该不该改还是该改。MGI 是换一条通向改变的路径——**curiosity 和 judgment 互斥**：你对一件事 judgmental 的时候，你就没法真的对它 curious。"

</div>

</div>

<div class="mt-6 p-5 rounded border-l-4 border-blue-500 bg-blue-50 bg-opacity-40">

### Dr. Becky 的一句副产品

"我开始 MGI 以后意识到一件事：**我又开始喜欢我的孩子了**。"

"parents 睡前真正让自己受不了的，不是孩子白天的行为，而是他们用什么词在脑子里描述自己的孩子——描述用得越久，'喜欢' 就越被磨掉。"

</div>

<div class="mt-4 text-sm opacity-70">
放到工作场景上是同一件事：你每天晚上在心里怎么形容你那个"最难搞"的同事，决定了第二天的你是什么样的 manager。
</div>

# 坏行为的底层公式

<div class="mt-3 p-3 rounded border-l-4 border-orange-500 bg-orange-50 bg-opacity-30 italic text-sm">

"kids are born with all the feelings and none of the skills to manage feelings."

</div>

<div class="mt-2 p-3 rounded border-l-4 border-red-500 bg-red-50 bg-opacity-30 italic text-sm">

"Bad behavior at any age can basically be reduced to feelings that overpower skills."

</div>

<div class="mt-2 p-3 rounded bg-blue-50 border-l-4 border-blue-500 not-italic text-sm">

**推论**：behavior 不是问题本身，而是问题的 manifestation。真正的问题是"这个人缺一项能力来处理内部正在发生的东西"。如果这个 skill 存在并能被 access，行为会改变——但那是结果，不是干预的位置。

</div>

<div class="mt-2 text-xs opacity-70">
Dr. Becky 本科时是 reward-punishment-timeout 流派——"I loved it because I have a very healthy left brain."——后来才发现这和她在私人诊所里帮 35 岁成年人改变的方法完全 at odds。
</div>

# 游泳类比：没人被骂着学会游泳

<div class="mt-6 p-5 rounded border-l-4 border-blue-500 bg-blue-50 bg-opacity-40 italic text-lg">

"it always has struck me with kids that we don't punish them into learning how to swim."

</div>

<div class="mt-4 text-sm opacity-70 not-italic">
"假设你付了钱让孩子去上游泳课，教练说'这孩子行为不端，我不伺候了，把他关回房间直到他学会游泳为止'——这听上去就很荒唐。但我们天天这样对孩子做情绪管理。"
</div>

<div class="mt-6 grid grid-cols-2 gap-4">

<div class="p-4 rounded bg-red-50 border-l-4 border-red-500">

### 常见反应

把孩子关进房间 / 给成人 PIP，期待他"自己想明白"。

Dr. Becky: "Nobody learns new skills by being sent to their room. Nobody learns new skills by adding shame."

</div>

<div class="p-4 rounded bg-green-50 border-l-4 border-green-500">

### 正确的顺序

1. **设边界**——不允许行为继续
2. **教那项缺失的 skill**——像教练一样
3. 短期行为会变，长期 **massively 变**

</div>

</div>

# Resilient Rebels：控制欲是核心恐惧

<div class="mt-4 grid grid-cols-2 gap-6">

<div>

### 孩子真正能控制的两件事

"Our kids当他们小的时候，他们整整一生只控制两件事: **what goes into their body and what comes out of their body.**"

这就是为什么吃饭和 potty 会变成战场——你一旦"踏进去"，人家就会用一切办法把你顶出去。

<div class="mt-4 p-3 rounded bg-orange-50 border-l-4 border-orange-500 text-sm">

**Dr. Becky 起的名字**：**"resilient rebels"**——温顺程度 0，core fear 是 loss of control，你一凑近他的 domain，他就 double down。

</div>

</div>

<div>

<div class="p-4 rounded bg-blue-50 border-l-4 border-blue-500">

### 同款成年人：改会议时间的地狱

"你只是想把一个会议时间往后挪半小时。对方的反应像是你朝他心脏捅了一刀。"

</div>

<div class="mt-3 p-4 rounded bg-purple-50 border-l-4 border-purple-500">

### 可能真正在发生的事

- 最近被跳过一次升职
- 刚换了新 manager
- 他的休假被拒
- 表面显化成"我不愿意改会议时间"

</div>

<div class="mt-2 p-2 rounded bg-green-50 border-l-4 border-green-500 text-xs">

**可落地**：明确划 domain——"这几件事你 own；这几件我会深度参与"。**名命名你的 intention**。

</div>

</div>

</div>

# Sturdy Leader：三种机长广播

<div class="mt-4 text-sm opacity-80">

飞机颠簸中，乘客尖叫。机长开广播有三种说法——

</div>

<div class="mt-3 space-y-2 text-sm">

<div class="p-2 rounded bg-red-50 border-l-4 border-red-500">

**1. 斥责型**："Stop screaming. You're making this flight awful for everyone and you're distracting me."
→ 乘客更怕了——机长情绪被尖叫影响了。

</div>

<div class="p-2 rounded bg-orange-50 border-l-4 border-orange-500">

**2. 过度纠正型**："Does anyone want to fly the plane?"
→ 育儿界最近的 overcorrection：孩子一闹情绪，情绪就决定决策。

</div>

<div class="p-2 rounded bg-green-50 border-l-4 border-green-500">

**3. Sturdy 型**："This turbulence, though it scares you, it doesn't scare me. See you when we land."
→ **承认你的情绪 + 守住自己的情绪**。

</div>

</div>

# 同一个公式：工作场景版本

<div class="mt-4 p-5 rounded border-l-4 border-blue-500 bg-blue-50 bg-opacity-30">

<div class="text-sm opacity-70 mb-2">假设场景：你宣布一个不受欢迎的政策调整（假期天数、回办公室要求、重组）——</div>

"Look, I want to share something that I know some people are going to have a reaction to. Here's the new policy. I get it. This is a big change. You're right. This does make things hard in a certain way. And the way you're feeling makes sense. Here's why we're doing it. I know we're going to get through it and I have faith in us to weather this turbulence."

</div>

<div class="mt-6 grid grid-cols-2 gap-4">

<div class="p-4 rounded bg-gray-50 border border-gray-200">

### Sturdy 的两个动作

1. **看到**对方的情绪是真实的
2. **不被**对方的情绪淹没

</div>

<div class="p-4 rounded bg-purple-50 border-l-4 border-purple-500">

### 为什么人会松一口气

"I feel this deep breath because my worry is contained."

**这个领导没有被我的焦虑绑架**——这让焦虑反而可以安全地存在。

</div>

# Boundary vs Request：一个重定义

<div class="mt-4 p-4 rounded border-l-4 border-blue-500 bg-blue-50 bg-opacity-30 italic">

"Boundaries are what you tell someone else you will do. And it requires the other person to do nothing. making a request. It's not a boundary."

</div>

<div class="mt-4 space-y-3 not-italic">

<div class="p-3 rounded bg-red-50 border-l-4 border-red-500">

**常见的"伪边界"**：
"I'm telling you, don't press the elevator buttons."
→ 这不是边界——它依赖你两岁孩子的配合。

</div>

<div class="p-3 rounded bg-green-50 border-l-4 border-green-500">

**真正的边界**：
"I'm going to stand between you and the buttons. I'm not going to let you press them."
→ 这是 **我要做的事**，不需要他做任何事。

</div>

<div class="p-3 rounded bg-purple-50 border-l-4 border-purple-500">

**Dr. Becky 的核心诊断**：
"you're giving all of your power to your 2-year-old."
工作里同理——你让 senior dev 决定这个代码要不要合并，但你才是那个有责任决定的人。

</div>

# 抱孩子去车里：一个完整 boundary 的样子

<div class="mt-4 text-sm opacity-70">Lenny 的实际提问：孩子不肯去车，我说"我要抱你去了"——这还是在要求他做事吗？</div>

<div class="mt-4 grid grid-cols-2 gap-6">

<div>

<div class="p-4 rounded bg-blue-50 border-l-4 border-blue-500">

### Dr. Becky 给的完整话术

"Look, it looks like it's hard for you to get to the car. I don't really want to do this, but I'm going to turn around. I'm going to take a deep breath. And if by the time I turn around, you're still here. I'm going to pick you up. I'm going to carry you to the car. And **even if you're crying and kicking and screaming, I'm still going to do that**, sweetie."

</div>

</div>

<div>

<div class="p-4 rounded bg-green-50 border-l-4 border-green-500">

### 重要的 reframe

"I'm going to turn around and you're going to be like 'I'm ready now, Dad.'"

**不要期待这个**。孩子不会因为你说得好就立刻听话。他们是在学游戏规则，不是在占你便宜。

</div>

<div class="mt-3 p-3 rounded bg-orange-50 border-l-4 border-orange-500 text-sm">

### 最反直觉的一点

新 boundary 一定会触发 **tantrum and protest**。

Dr. Becky: "**see that as a sign that you're successfully setting a boundary**. 'This is going according to plan.'"

</div>

# Resilience over Happiness

<div class="mt-3 p-4 rounded border-l-4 border-red-500 bg-red-50 bg-opacity-40 italic">

"Optimizing for happiness in childhood is the quickest way to build anxiety and fragility in adulthood. Hard stop."

</div>

<div class="mt-3 grid grid-cols-2 gap-3 text-sm">

<div class="p-3 rounded bg-blue-50 border-l-4 border-blue-500">

### 为什么

孩子幼年学的不是"怎么让自己一直开心"，而是 **"what range of experiences am I able to cope with?"**

如果只教他 optimize for happiness，他学到的是 **any discomfort must be brought to zero**。

</div>

<div class="p-3 rounded bg-purple-50 border-l-4 border-purple-500">

### 反讽

成年之后真正盖住 happiness 的，是你管理不了 disappointment / jealousy / 感觉 less than 的能力。

**"our kids can only learn to tolerate the feelings we tolerate in them."**

</div>

</div>

<div class="mt-3 p-3 rounded bg-green-50 border-l-4 border-green-500 text-sm">

**工作里同构**：怕给 junior 反馈 → 6 个月后被迫 PIP → 他回头问"你为什么不早点告诉我"。前几个月你在 **building the foundation for the whole building** —— 越早 optimize for comfort，building 越 fragile。

</div>

# "I believe you" + "I believe in you"

# 演练：一个孩子 + 一个同事

I'd be nervous if you weren't nervous.

**And** the reason we think you can take on this project is because we know you can figure this out. I'm here to answer questions. Yes, this is hard. And I also know you can work through this. And the feeling at the end—I don't want to take that feeling away from you."

# "Leave Me Alone"：deeply feeling kids

<div class="mt-4 text-sm opacity-70">Dr. Becky 正在写第二本绘本，名字叫《Leave Me Alone》——是关于她所谓 "deeply feeling kids" 的——</div>

# 明天就能用：一句话的 360

<div class="mt-4 text-sm opacity-70">临走前 Dr. Becky 说她最近问孩子的一个问题，效果惊人——</div>

<div class="mt-6 p-5 rounded border-l-4 border-orange-500 bg-orange-50 bg-opacity-40 italic text-xl">

"If I could do one thing different this week to be a better parent to you, what would it be?"

</div>

<div class="mt-6 grid grid-cols-2 gap-6">

<div class="p-4 rounded bg-blue-50 border-l-4 border-blue-500">

### 关键动作

- 问完之后 **just get ready to receive**
- 孩子可能说傻话（"you could let me watch more TV"）
- 也可能说伤你的话（"you cannot be on your phone as much"）
- **都接住**："Okay, thank you for telling me that."

</div>

### 换成工作

"What if my manager asked me that question? Wouldn't I immediately just feel better about my manager anyway just from the asking of the question?"

Dr. Becky 原话：**这个问题本身就有治疗效应**，不管答案是什么。

这其实反转了这期整个方向：不是"把工作思维带回家"——而是"把家里最脆弱、最坦诚的那种问法带进工作"。
