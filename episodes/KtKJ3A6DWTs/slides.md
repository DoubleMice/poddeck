# 核心比喻：low heart rate

礼仪不是"多有教养"，是一种**身体信号**。

房间里的对手能读出你的心跳。

<div class="mt-6 p-4 rounded border-l-4 border-blue-500 bg-blue-50 bg-opacity-40 italic">

"Etiquette is a skill for how to show up in a room with a low heart rate."

</div>

<div class="mt-4 text-sm opacity-70">

所有后面的战术 —— 早到、不追问、先让别人点菜、摄像头开着 —— 全部都是把 heart rate 往下拉的具体做法。

# 场景：Kleiner Perkins 的 holiday party

<div class="mt-4 text-base">

你 22 岁，第一家公司，站在 Kleiner Perkins 的圣诞 party 里。全世界的 VC 和 CEO 都在这间屋子里。

你脑子里全是：**"这是我的机会。我要谈下这个人，连上那个人。"**

</div>

<div class="mt-6 grid grid-cols-2 gap-4">

<div class="p-4 rounded border-l-4 border-red-500 bg-red-50 bg-opacity-40">

### ❌ 像只 Energizer Bunny

转换率至上，每个对话都是一次 pitch。

Sam: "If you show up like a little Energizer Bunny, you're going to scare [people] off."

</div>

<div class="p-4 rounded border-l-4 border-green-500 bg-green-50 bg-opacity-40">

### ✅ self-confidence + calm of abundance

"This is **part** of the story. This is **not the entire** story."

这不是你唯一的机会。你以后还会遇到这个人。

</div>

</div>

<div class="mt-5 text-sm opacity-60">

—— Sam 反复提醒：22 岁时你看什么都是 scarcity，但场上读到的是你的 scarcity，不是你的野心。

</div>

# scarcity → abundance

一个心态切换，其他所有规则都是推论。

<div class="mt-4 p-3 rounded border-l-4 border-purple-500 bg-purple-50 bg-opacity-40 text-sm">

Lenny 的概括："你可以不懂这些也成功 —— 但你是在为自己制造一个不必要的 uphill battle。"

</div>

<div class="mt-4 text-sm">

Sam 的例外条款：

如果你带下来的是 **mana from heaven**（凭空造出下一个 Google），那你基本可以 get away with anything —— 但那是 0.1% 的创业者，不是你。

# 为什么这在 2026 反而更重要

<div class="grid grid-cols-2 gap-5 mt-4">

<div class="p-5 rounded border-l-4 border-blue-500 bg-blue-50 bg-opacity-40">

### 软件被商品化

当"写一个应用"变便宜、门槛消失，差异化越来越不在代码上，而在 **谁能被信任**。

Sam: "technology is no longer some cute sideshow, but is like a major deal. People are worried about losing their jobs."

</div>

<div class="p-5 rounded border-l-4 border-orange-500 bg-orange-50 bg-opacity-40">

### 信任变成硬通货

"You're asking people to trust you with their data, trust you with their business."

在这个 context 里，**meet people where they're at, mirror expected behaviors** —— 这些"软技能"是实打实的工具。

</div>

</div>

<div class="mt-6 p-4 rounded border border-gray-200 text-sm italic">

反讽：硅谷这么多年的叙事是"别管这些，专注 product"。Sam 的立场是：**I'm wearing a t-shirt, I'm not exactly known as the most high etiquette person in the world —— but I do know the rules.**

</div>

# 进屋的 4 条基础规则

<div class="grid grid-cols-2 gap-4 mt-4">

<div class="p-4 rounded bg-blue-50 border-l-4 border-blue-500">

### be early（但别太早）

10-15 min OK，早半小时就在接待处坐着反而让对方心里不安 —— **"you're 6 coffees deep with the receptionist"**。

</div>

<div class="p-4 rounded bg-green-50 border-l-4 border-green-500">

### 迟到了，就简单 apologize 过去

别搞成 five alarm fire。"You come in flustered and all over the place —— it's okay. We understand."

</div>

<div class="p-4 rounded bg-orange-50 border-l-4 border-orange-500">

### 握手要 firm，不要碾碎对方的手

"Practice on your friends, right?"—— Sam 认真说的，不是开玩笑。

</div>

<div class="p-4 rounded bg-purple-50 border-l-4 border-purple-500">

### **repeat names back**

"Hey, Lenny, it's great to meet you."——不仅帮你记住，还 signal 了"你不是我的一张名片"。

</div>

</div>

<div class="mt-6 p-4 rounded border border-gray-300 text-sm">

**反直觉**：如果对方迟到，**不要** make them feel bad。你赢不到什么，还让接下来 25 分钟的会议都处在错的基调上。

</div>

# 眼神：签到的是 effort，不是结果

<div class="mt-4 text-base">

在一个六个窗口同时开着的年代，**眼神接触**是最贵的礼物之一 —— 不是因为它难，而是因为它稀缺。

</div>

<div class="mt-6 p-5 rounded border-l-4 border-blue-500 bg-blue-50 bg-opacity-40 italic text-lg">

"What matters in some ways is the **signaling of the effort** as much as the actual thing."

</div>

<div class="mt-6 grid grid-cols-2 gap-4">

<div class="p-4 rounded bg-gray-50 border border-gray-200">

### 有 neurodivergence 怎么办

"There is grace in this, to a point."

signal effort 比 perform confidence 更重要。"If you have trouble with this but you're really trying, that goes a long way."

</div>

<div class="p-4 rounded bg-gray-50 border border-gray-200">

### 对方的 partner 在场

"**Introduce them first.**"Bring them into the conversation。

这是 Sam 反复用到的 costly signal —— 它成本低，但 signal 出你有没有在意场里的所有人。

</div>

</div>

# 名字的 hack："let it hang"

<div class="mt-2 text-xs opacity-80">

Sam 承认他有 **clinical** 的人脸识别障碍（"runs in our family"）。

</div>

<div class="mt-3 p-3 rounded border-l-4 border-orange-500 bg-orange-50 bg-opacity-40 text-sm">

### 标准做法

"Lenny, please meet my wife Jessica."

</div>

<div class="mt-3 p-3 rounded border-l-4 border-green-500 bg-green-50 bg-opacity-40 text-sm">

### 记不起对方的名字时

"**Jessica, I want to introduce you** ..." 看对方的眼睛，停一拍。

对方几乎一定会接上 "I'm Bob. Nice to meet you." —— 你无痛地回收了名字。

</div>

# "great to see you" vs "nice to meet you"

<div class="mt-4 text-base">

这一条单独拎出来，因为它回本速度最快。

</div>

<div class="mt-6 grid grid-cols-2 gap-5">

<div class="p-5 rounded border-l-4 border-red-400 bg-red-50 bg-opacity-40">

### "Nice to meet you"

只在**确实第一次见**时才安全。

说错了 → "We've met five times." → 尴尬值拉满。

</div>

<div class="p-5 rounded border-l-4 border-green-500 bg-green-50 bg-opacity-40">

### "Great to see you"

**不管见没见过都 work**。

Sam: 他太太第一次正经互动时，他说了"nice to see you"—— 她记得他们见过，他忘了。这事他至今在记。

</div>

</div>

<div class="mt-6 p-4 rounded bg-yellow-50 border border-yellow-200 text-sm">

<strong>Sam 的 tiebreaker</strong>: "People aren't dumb." 如果你对所有人都说"nice to see you"，总有人会意识到 —— 但那没关系。<strong>真正 not fine 的是</strong> "It's so nice to **meet** you" 对一个已经见过六次的人。

</div>

# 对话 = ping-pong，不是 monologue

<div class="mt-2 text-base">

Sam 反复用这个比喻。**一来一回**，不是"你 serve 10 个球"，也不是"你独白 4 分钟"。

</div>

<div class="mt-4 p-4 rounded border-l-4 border-orange-500 bg-orange-50 bg-opacity-40 text-sm">

### 关于 questions 的陷阱

问问题是好的 —— 但**一连 6 个就是 inquisition**。

"Sometimes I feel this. You'll meet someone and you feel like it's the inquisition ... like all they're trying to do is extract information."

</div>

<div class="mt-3 p-4 rounded border-l-4 border-blue-500 bg-blue-50 bg-opacity-40 text-sm">

### give-to-get 的最简表述

对方 serve 一个点/一个故事 → 你接住 → 再打回去。

That's openness. That's not single-player.

</div>

# leave them wanting more

<div class="mt-4 text-base">

对话的 real goal 不是"把你的信息塞进对方脑子"，是让他们走开以后想：

</div>

<div class="mt-5 p-5 rounded border-l-4 border-green-500 bg-green-50 bg-opacity-40 italic text-lg">

"Wow, that was a really interesting person. I'd love to hear from them again."

</div>

<div class="mt-4 p-5 rounded border-l-4 border-purple-500 bg-purple-50 bg-opacity-40 italic text-lg">

（更好的版本）"That's a really interesting person —— Lenny, you should talk to Sam."

</div>

<div class="mt-6 grid grid-cols-2 gap-4 text-sm">

<div class="p-3 rounded bg-gray-50 border border-gray-200">

**所以 Sam 的推论：**

"Knowing when to excuse yourself gracefully 和 knowing when to enter 一样重要。"

</div>

<div class="p-3 rounded bg-gray-50 border border-gray-200">

**这又回到 abundance:**

"This isn't your one shot. You'll have other opportunities."

</div>

</div>

# 遇到名人：既不 sycophantic 也不 ridiculous

<div class="mt-4 grid grid-cols-2 gap-5">

<div class="p-5 rounded border-l-4 border-red-500 bg-red-50 bg-opacity-40">

### ❌ ridiculous

"Hi, I'm Sam. And who are you?" 对面是 Mark Zuckerberg，全世界都认识。

这是在装。装是 high heart rate 的 tell。

</div>

<div class="p-5 rounded border-l-4 border-red-500 bg-red-50 bg-opacity-40">

### ❌ sycophantic

"You're the most important person I've ever met."

你不会加分，只会让对方尴尬。

</div>

</div>

<div class="mt-5 p-5 rounded border-l-4 border-green-500 bg-green-50 bg-opacity-40">

### ✅ 名人也是人，你在玩 iterative game

"This is not my only opportunity to meet Mark"——哪怕事实上就是。

Sam 的反面教材："I need your email address and phone number." 对方没 offer，你就别主动伸手要。

</div>

<div class="mt-5 text-sm opacity-70">

核心：你想让对方走开以后觉得 —— **"That was a pretty nice person. Maybe I want to talk to them again."**

</div>

# humor：用，但当成武器用

<div class="mt-4 grid grid-cols-2 gap-5">

<div class="p-5 rounded border-l-4 border-blue-500 bg-blue-50 bg-opacity-40">

### 为什么 humor 很强

"The ultimate demonstration of comfort in a space is to tell a joke that's **a little over the line or a little off color but not too off color**."

—— 它是 mastery 的 costly signal。

</div>

<div class="p-5 rounded border-l-4 border-orange-500 bg-orange-50 bg-opacity-40">

### 为什么也很危险

"A joke that no one laughs at is a **huge risk maneuver**. You've failed."

—— 所以要 **sparingly and smartly**。

</div>

</div>

<div class="mt-5 p-5 rounded border-l-4 border-purple-500 bg-purple-50 bg-opacity-40">

### Sam 的具体做法：**a file of jokes**

"I have like a Bear note of my favorite jokes and they're **loosely ranked from least offensive to most offensive**."

<div class="mt-2 text-sm opacity-70">
—— 建议你也有一个。Lenny 现场承认："I have zero jokes in my head."
</div>

</div>

<div class="mt-5 text-sm opacity-70">

补充：Self-deprecation 永远安全。取笑别人是 high-risk maneuver —— "the second it feels disparaging ... you're out."

</div>

# 关于穿衣：fit > brand

<div class="mt-3 grid grid-cols-3 gap-3 text-sm">

<div class="p-3 rounded border-l-4 border-blue-500 bg-blue-50 bg-opacity-40">

### 规则 1：dress one level up

"It's an easy way to win. Not two, not three, but **one**."

多半套，脱 jacket 可往下调；差半套，救不回来。

</div>

<div class="p-3 rounded border-l-4 border-green-500 bg-green-50 bg-opacity-40">

### 规则 2：fit is everything

"A well-fitting $20 shirt is way better than a misfitting $500 shirt."

普通人看不出价格，**几乎所有人都看得出 fit**。

</div>

<div class="p-3 rounded border-l-4 border-orange-500 bg-orange-50 bg-opacity-40">

### 规则 3：别戴 Rolex

"It's very **classless** ... you're not going to trick anyone."

贵但不合身的东西，signal 的是"在用钱买存在感"。

</div>

</div>

<div class="mt-4 p-3 rounded border border-gray-300 text-sm">

**Sam 的作弊键**：不确定 dress code？**just ask**。问"今天大家穿什么"比穿错便宜得多——而且 "asking shows a level of confidence and calm and humility."

</div>

# tuxedo Tuesdays：know the rules to break them

<div class="mt-4 text-base">

Sam 大学毕业第一份工作在 Bain（consulting）。Dress code 写明 business casual。

他发现一件事："**There was a minimum dress code, but there wasn't a maximum dress code.**"

</div>

<div class="mt-6 p-6 rounded border-l-4 border-purple-500 bg-purple-50 bg-opacity-40">

### 于是 —— Tuxedo Tuesdays

所有 associates 周二穿 tuxedo 去上班。

**后果**：客户会议不敢带他们。

"They would never take us to client meetings in tuxedos."

</div>

<div class="mt-6 p-4 rounded border border-gray-300 text-sm italic">

这是一个更一般的原则：**"Know the rules, but don't always follow them."**

破规则要带 "a lot of cultural understanding." 否则就不是反叛，只是 clueless。

</div>

---

# hygiene：不要让任何东西 memorable

<div class="mt-4 p-5 rounded border-l-4 border-orange-500 bg-orange-50 bg-opacity-40 italic text-lg">

"Don't smell like [expletive]."

</div>

<div class="mt-4 p-5 rounded border-l-4 border-blue-500 bg-blue-50 bg-opacity-40">

### 香水也一样

"You shouldn't smell like you just doused yourself in perfume ... **your scent should not be noticeable in any direction.**"

</div>

<div class="mt-5 grid grid-cols-2 gap-4 text-sm">

<div class="p-3 rounded bg-gray-50 border border-gray-200">

**overdress vs underdress**

都是在 signal "no respect for the room."

tuxedo in business casual = show-off. 拖鞋去 black-tie = contempt.

</div>

<div class="p-3 rounded bg-gray-50 border border-gray-200">

**overarching 原则**

你的穿着、香味、napkin 位置、tipping 数额——**任何 memorable 的东西都是你的成本**。

目标是 stay out of the way，让 conversation 跑。

</div>

</div>

# Dining：规则很多，心态很少

<div class="mt-4 grid grid-cols-2 gap-4">

<div class="p-4 rounded bg-blue-50 border-l-4 border-blue-500">

### tip a lot

"10-20% is the minimum ... 20% feels like the minimum. 30% sometimes."

目标：**不要让你的 tip 成为别人记得的那件事。**

</div>

<div class="p-4 rounded bg-green-50 border-l-4 border-green-500">

### don't order first

"The more you let someone else set the tone and then match that tone, the better."

Starters 有没有？酒喝不喝？—— 先看主人动作。

</div>

<div class="p-4 rounded bg-orange-50 border-l-4 border-orange-500">

### don't order the most expensive thing

"Does it really matter? No, they don't really care. But **they notice**."

signal 的是：你对价格不敏感。

</div>

<div class="p-4 rounded bg-purple-50 border-l-4 border-purple-500">

### always offer to pay

"99% of the time they'll say 'I got this.' But 那个动作很重要。"

例外：$10k 的酒单 / billionaire 已经主动点了特别贵的酒。

</div>

</div>

<div class="mt-5 p-4 rounded bg-yellow-50 border border-yellow-200 text-sm">

**Sam 的 micro-story**：跟名人吃饭、你 offer 买单时，要 tip 得**像他们平时那样**——"They're gonna tip like 100% of the bill." 你要是按 20% 走，反而尬。

</div>

# B for bread, D for drinks

<div class="mt-2 text-sm">

坐下 round table，不知道哪杯是自己的？双手比一个 <strong>"OK"</strong>：

- **左手** = "b" → bread plate 在左
- **右手** = "d" → drink 在右

</div>

<div class="mt-3 p-3 rounded border-l-4 border-orange-500 bg-orange-50 bg-opacity-40 text-xs">

### Sam 真的会 nitpick 的

"**The knife blade goes in** when you put the knife down."

</div>

<div class="mt-3 p-3 rounded border-l-4 border-blue-500 bg-blue-50 bg-opacity-40 text-xs">

### napkin goes in your lap

不是脖子上，不是旁边椅子上。"I don't think you want your napkin placement to be memorable."

</div>

# Calendarly：Sam 的 single-handed 战争

<div class="mt-4 text-base">

Sam 半开玩笑地说自己是 Calendarly 月度 KPI 的最大功臣 —— 他在 Twitter 上骂 Calendarly 太 disrespectful，骂到对方董事会给他发消息。

</div>

<div class="mt-5 grid grid-cols-2 gap-4">

<div class="p-4 rounded border-l-4 border-red-500 bg-red-50 bg-opacity-40">

### 为什么他讨厌 Calendarly 作为默认

"The default in most situations, especially if you recognize the power hierarchy, **the busyness hierarchy**—if you're the less senior person, if you are the less busy person, you should let the other person tell you when they're free and then make it work on your end."

</div>

<div class="p-4 rounded border-l-4 border-green-500 bg-green-50 bg-opacity-40">

### 什么时候可以发 Calendarly

你是那场关系里更 busy / 更资深的一方，并且**你给的是真 options**。

"If you're going to use a scheduling agent or something, it just needs to actually have real options."

</div>

</div>

<div class="mt-5 p-4 rounded bg-purple-50 border-l-4 border-purple-500 text-sm">

**推论**：rescheduling 是可以的，但此时你"基本上 within reason need to make it work for them."

</div>

# 尊重 EAs：the #1 way to look classless

<div class="mt-4 p-5 rounded border-l-4 border-orange-500 bg-orange-50 bg-opacity-40">

"The number one way to look extremely classless is to not respect people who are helping the other person."

</div>

<div class="mt-5 text-base">

Sam 卖公司给 Airbnb 的时候，他们的 banker **刻意**花精力跟 Airbnb 的 EA 和 office manager 建关系 —— "it helps you get the thing."

但 Sam 也补了一条："**不要 overdo it.** 你要是给 EA 送花，那动作就太 transactional 了。"

</div>

<div class="mt-6 grid grid-cols-2 gap-4 text-sm">

# Email 基本面

<div class="mt-4 grid grid-cols-2 gap-4">

<div class="p-4 rounded bg-blue-50 border-l-4 border-blue-500">

### 1. 假设对方很忙

"If you write an email, imagine you're receiving it. How does it feel?"

10 段文字 = "I'm going to spend 10 minutes talking at you."

<div class="p-4 rounded bg-green-50 border-l-4 border-green-500">

### 2. Sam 信 email SLA

"I don't owe you a 12-page essay, but **I do owe at least an acknowledgement quickly**."

不是所有人这么想，但 Sam 这么想。

### 3. emoji 慎用

"Emojis almost feel like jokes to me —— tell them at your own risk."

特别信号：满屏 emoji 容易让人觉得 "generated by AI, because GPT loves emojis."

<div class="p-4 rounded bg-red-50 border-l-4 border-red-500">

### 4. 最坏的失误

Sam 的真实翻车："to check the spelling of someone's name, I put them in the To: field and hit send."

——"That's not even an etiquette thing. That's just like being smart."

# TO 和 CC 的语言

<div class="mt-2 text-base">

这段是 Sam 真正觉得很多人"看不到"的 layer。

</div>

<div class="mt-4 p-3 rounded border-l-4 border-blue-500 bg-blue-50 bg-opacity-40 text-sm">

### TO: 顺序是 signal

"If you're sending an email and the first person is the **assistant** and the fourth person is the **CEO**, you've probably done it wrong."

"The CC line means: hey, you should have a copy of this. **This is not really to you. I'm not expecting an immediate response.**"

（这不止礼仪，是不让自己社会性死亡。）

# meetings：camera on, closet closed

# small talk = TCP handshake

<div class="mt-4 text-base">

Lenny 在 Twitter 上看到有人把 small talk 比作 **"the TCP IP ACK handshake"**。

Sam 加了一个自己版本：**"modem crash."**

</div>

<div class="mt-6 p-6 rounded border-l-4 border-blue-500 bg-blue-50 bg-opacity-40 italic text-lg">

"Imagine the modem crash from when we were kids on a 14.4 ... that old modem crash. That's what small talk is. We're trying to hit the wavelengths."

</div>

<div class="mt-6 grid grid-cols-2 gap-4 text-sm">

<div class="p-4 rounded bg-gray-50 border border-gray-200">

### 所以该怎么用

开场的 "how was your weekend" / "weather is nice" **不是浪费时间** —— 它是在 sync 两边的频率。

它显得 transparent 也没事，因为所有人都知道这是 handshake。

</div>

<div class="p-4 rounded bg-gray-50 border border-gray-200">

### 它真正在 signal 什么

"I know that we should start with a normal conversation."

你在 signal —— 我懂人类的协议栈，不是一来就 SYN/ACK 到 business 的机器。

</div>

</div>

# 结束：Irish goodbye 经常是对的

<div class="mt-4 text-base">

**conversation 结束的时候，别杵着。**

Sam 描述的最糟场景："the conversation ends and the person just stands there."

</div>

<div class="mt-5 grid grid-cols-2 gap-5">

<div class="p-5 rounded border-l-4 border-blue-500 bg-blue-50 bg-opacity-40">

### 对话级：读信号

"If someone says in a conversation 'I'm going to go grab a drink' —— most of the time that is **not** an invitation to say 'I'd love one too' and follow them to the bar."

另一个技巧：把对方**交接**给另一个人——"Have you met Steve?"

</div>

<div class="p-5 rounded border-l-4 border-purple-500 bg-purple-50 bg-opacity-40">

### 整场级：Irish goodbye

"In any large group setting I think an **Irish goodbye** is the best goodbye."

向主人 thank 一下就走。挨个 hug 告别 —— "it's too much."

</div>

</div>

<div class="mt-5 p-4 rounded border border-gray-300 text-sm">

别忘了 **thank you note**。不用长，不用夸张——"we met, I got something out of it, thanks for your time." 小成本，长复利。

</div>

# 整套东西的 meta-rule

<div class="mt-4 p-6 rounded border-l-4 border-purple-500 bg-purple-50 bg-opacity-40 italic text-lg">

"What you're signaling is: I've got this, we're on the same wavelength, my heart rate is low, I'm doing it properly, and I'm doing it **intuitively, almost**."

</div>

<div class="mt-3 text-sm">

所有规则指向同一个目标 —— **不占用你 80% 的脑子**。

</div>

<div class="mt-3 grid grid-cols-3 gap-3 text-xs">

<div class="p-3 rounded bg-gray-50 border border-gray-200">

### ✗ performing etiquette

"看着我多 proper"——heart rate 反而上去。

</div>

<div class="p-3 rounded bg-gray-50 border border-gray-200">

### ✗ ignoring etiquette

"这些都不重要"——给自己设 unnecessary uphill battles。

</div>

<div class="p-3 rounded bg-green-50 border border-green-200">

### ✓ intuiting etiquette

"同一波段上"——对方在 subconscious 里读出：**这人可信**。

</div>

</div>

<div class="mt-3 p-3 rounded bg-blue-50 border border-blue-200 text-xs italic">

Sam 书里的 TLDR 第一句：**"Remember the goal of all etiquette is essentially building trust and project genuine confidence."**

</div>

# Contrarian Corner：AI 公司 = seed 坟场

<div class="mt-2 text-base">

Lenny 问 Sam Contrarian Corner，他给了一个很具体的押注。

</div>

<div class="mt-4 p-4 rounded border-l-4 border-red-500 bg-red-50 bg-opacity-40">

"the seed venture capitalists who invest in companies that are branded as AI companies are going to lose an **impossibly large amount of money** in the coming years."

</div>

<div class="mt-4 text-sm">

**三条理由**：

Sam 拿 OpenAI 算账："Even at a **$500 billion market cap**, the seed investors have made something like **25 times their money**. That's ... middling at best seed investment for the company that is defining the moment."

# 但 Sam 不是反 AI

<div class="mt-4 p-5 rounded border-l-4 border-green-500 bg-green-50 bg-opacity-40">

"Not using AI in your startup is the equivalent of not using the cloud in like 2010 or like not using the internet in like 2000. It would be **insane**."

</div>

<div class="mt-5 text-base">

**他投的类型**（作者概括）：不是"我们是 AI X"，而是"因为 AI 改变了世界，所以需要这样的生意"。

</div>

<div class="mt-5 grid grid-cols-3 gap-3">

<div class="p-4 rounded bg-blue-50 border-l-4 border-blue-500 text-sm">

### Voight-Kampff 类

**Sublime Security / Outtake** 这类 —— Sam 引 *Blade Runner* 里的 "Voight-Kampff test"：are you real, or are you a bot?

AI 造了海量的"是不是真人"问题，谁解这个问题，谁赚钱。

</div>

<div class="p-4 rounded bg-orange-50 border-l-4 border-orange-500 text-sm">

### 文化冲击类

AI 改变了 dating、social、trust —— 比如 June Date，用 ChatGPT history 匹配两个人，不是"AI girlfriend"而是更好的 Tinder。

</div>

<div class="p-4 rounded bg-purple-50 border-l-4 border-purple-500 text-sm">

### 被 AI 反噬的生意

传统行业被 AI 重写 —— 这里有好生意，**but they're not AI businesses, they're businesses that will be disrupted in interesting ways**。

</div>

</div>

<div class="mt-5 p-3 rounded bg-yellow-50 border border-yellow-200 text-sm italic">

Sam 的大 framing: "You're going to win **because of something else**. AI is going to be a **propellant** to it."
